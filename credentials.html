College Credit Opportunity

Students can earn up to 7 college credit hours through AP exams.

These credits can help students save on tuition, graduate earlier, and move into higher-level college courses with a stronger foundation in technology and computer science.

  • AP Computer Science Principles – 3 credit hours
  • AP Computer Science A – 4 credit hours
Why AP matters

Academic momentum for college pathways

AP Computer Science Principles often counts toward general education or introductory computing coursework, while AP Computer Science A often aligns with core programming requirements for computer science majors.

This creates a valuable bridge between high school learning and college-level expectations.

Why certifications matter

Workforce-ready value alongside academic rigor

Industry credentials help students show that their skills are practical and measurable. They add value to applications, resumes, scholarship materials, and future opportunities in technical fields.

Together, certifications and AP coursework create a strong dual benefit: college readiness and career readiness.

4-Year Course Sequence

The Trimble Tech Computer Science Pathway

A structured progression that helps students develop computing fundamentals, programming expertise, academic readiness, and portfolio-level work.

9
9th Grade

Computer Science 1

Students begin their pathway by learning foundational computing concepts, logical thinking, and structured problem-solving. This course builds early confidence with technology systems and introduces the mindset needed for more advanced programming courses.

Computational Thinking
Digital Problem-Solving
Introductory Coding Concepts
10
10th Grade

AP Computer Science Principles

This course offers a broad exploration of computing. Students examine how data, the internet, programming, and digital systems shape the modern world. It emphasizes both technical foundations and the impact of technology in society.

Data + Internet Concepts
Programming Basics
AP Exam Preparation
11
11th Grade

AP Computer Science A

Students dive deeply into Java programming and object-oriented design. The course focuses on algorithms, classes, methods, arrays, control flow, and problem decomposition. It is a major step toward college-level computer science.

Java Programming
Algorithms + Logic
Object-Oriented Design
12
12th Grade

Computer Science Independent Study

Seniors apply their learning through advanced projects, research, and portfolio development. Independent Study supports student voice, self-direction, and deeper exploration of future pathways in software, cybersecurity, IT, engineering, and other technology fields.

Capstone Projects
Portfolio Development
College + Career Preparation

Student Experience

What learning looks like in the pathway

Students do more than study concepts. They practice building, presenting, iterating, and applying what they learn in meaningful ways.

Project-Based Learning

Students create websites, programs, coding exercises, and final products that show their progress over time.

Presentation Skills

Students explain their code, defend design decisions, and communicate technical ideas clearly.

Problem-Solving Practice

Students learn how to break complex tasks into manageable steps and debug thoughtfully.

Future Readiness

Students build habits and experiences that support college majors, internships, and tech-related careers.
